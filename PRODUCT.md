# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Fotbarin is for Gen Z users who want to create cute, fast photobooth strips directly in the browser, especially for selfies, bestie/couple shots, fandom-adjacent content, and social sharing. Users may be on mobile in casual settings, want a result in under a minute, and should not need login for the core shooting flow. Future creator users will design and publish reusable frames.

## Product Purpose

Fotbarin lets users open a browser camera, capture a short sequence, choose a frame/template, and download a polished photo strip without uploading personal photos. Success means the app feels trustworthy, quick, privacy-first, and visually desirable enough that users want to share the result.

## Brand Personality

Clean cute, privacy-trustworthy, and socially playful. The interface should feel friendly and Gen Z-aware without looking like a generic pastel sticker template. The product should be cute in outcomes and details, but mature and reliable in the workflow.

## Anti-references

Avoid AI-slop patterns: excessive decorative cards, generic pastel gradients, random icons, fake SaaS copy, overdone sticker shadows, identical icon-card grids, and typography that feels like a generated landing page. Avoid making the tool feel childish, cluttered, or visually loud at the expense of camera/export usability.

## Design Principles

1. Keep the shooting flow calm and focused; decoration must never compete with the camera.
2. Make privacy visible through precise product language and stable interaction, not fear-based banners.
3. Use cuteness as craft: proportion, spacing, gentle color, and output quality before stickers.
4. Preserve standard product affordances so users trust the tool immediately.
5. Every screen should have a clear job: shoot, choose, price, login, or browse.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for WCAG AA contrast for body text and controls. Support keyboard focus states, reduced motion, clear labels, mobile touch targets, and readable text over tinted surfaces. Avoid relying on color alone for selected/error states.
